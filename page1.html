Ohm's Law

Ohm's Law describes the relationship between voltage, current, and resistance in an electrical circuit. It states that:

Voltage (V) = Current (I) × Resistance (R)
Current (I) = Voltage (V) / Resistance (R)
Resistance (R) = Voltage (V) / Current (I)

Where:

  • Voltage (V) is measured in volts (V).
  • Current (I) is measured in amperes (A).
  • Resistance (R) is measured in ohms (Ω).

Here's a table summarizing Ohm's Law:

Formula Description
V = I × R Voltage equals current multiplied by resistance.
I = V / R Current equals voltage divided by resistance.
R = V / I Resistance equals voltage divided by current.
